# like-human-do — round 2 worklog (goal-awareness)

Round 1 made the email human. Round 2 stops it being so human it drops the email's job. Trigger: on a chamber-offer email (see `regression/chamber.md`), round-1 guidance told the author to strip "the league-table boast" and "numbers paraded for status" — which was the exact payload (a 41,000 organic-follower achievement) that earns the offer a members' slot. Editing for warmth was undoing the persuasion.

## What was tested

Variants as minimal plugin copies under `variants/`, 0-pass compose, marker-tagged:

- **V0** — round-1 shipped guidance (control).
- **V2** — + goal pass (name the action; do not ask when the draft states it) + protect goal-bearing content (replaces the blunt strip-the-boast rule) + a "does it still do its job" check + a no-action guard.
- **V3** — V2 + a Carnegie "their side" step (speculate why the recipient acted; frame the offer to their motive). The exploratory arm.

Cases: the chamber offer (new; no fork — the agent was told at prompt level the context was in the 17th meeting under the business knowledge-capture, and had to retrieve it), plus distiller and organiser re-forked from session `67168ade` for regression. Scored by blind recipient-sim (informed persona) on human-ness and, for action emails, goal-power (how readily the reader would act), plus payload retention (experimenter greps the invariant facts; the skill is never told them) and goal-extraction-without-asking.

## Findings

- **Goal-awareness (V2) wins.** On the chamber email V2 kept all three payload facts (41,000 / all organic, no paid / about two years) plus the superlative the reader named as "the reason the talk would be worth attending," and the blind committee reader said "reply today." V0 dropped the superlative and softened "two years" to "since 2024," and the reader would act only "within a day or two." The original AI draft read as a quiet pitch (doubled "no sales pitch") and was "parked, on guard." So both edits beat the original on goal-power; goal-awareness beat the shipped guidance on payload retention.
- **The over-strip flaw is intermittent, not constant.** In one V0 run the boast survived; in another it was dropped. The shipped guidance *permitted* the loss; V2's explicit protect step removed the permission. That is enough reason to ship it.
- **No-action guard, found by regression.** An early V2 on the distiller (a no-action relationship email) manufactured a soft reciprocal-referral goal — "your visitors / our visitors, come for a ride" — and the reader sensed "a referral arrangement dressed as neighbourly." Fix: when the email needs no action, say so and lead with warmth, never reach for a business angle. After the fix the distiller read as "a real person who had a good chat," no transactional flag, on par with the round-1 golden.
- **Carnegie (V3) tested, not adopted.** It tied V2 on goal-power (both "reply today") and the reader did notice the perspective framing ("he gets what we're trying to do with these nights"), but it did not *beat* V2, and it softened the two-year span and leaked a reasoning line. Per the rule — perspective-taking ships only if it wins clearly — it was left out. No rename. The hypothesis that speculating *why* the organiser ran the session would outperform was not borne out; it matched.
- **Goal-extraction:** every run inferred the action from the email without asking. The no-ask check passed for all; it did not discriminate.

## Shipped

`skills/edit-email/like-human-do.md` gained: "The goal" (with the no-action guard), "Protect what does the job" (replacing the strip-the-boast clause in the offer rule), and "Does it still do its job." `SKILL.md` procedure now runs the goal pass before the connection/ask passes and the job-check before assembling. Carnegie not added.
